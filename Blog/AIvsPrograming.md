# Why Learn Programming in 2026?

> AI Writes Code, But Here’s Why You Still Need to Learn Programming in 2026

With AI tools becoming more powerful, many beginners wonder why they should still learn programming at all. After all, AI can already generate working code, explain concepts, and debug problems. But there are important reasons why learning to program is still essential.

## 1. AI Is Helpful, but Not Always Correct
Modern AI models can produce code quickly, but they are not perfectly accurate.
For example, one evaluation showed that LLAMA‑13B reached around 76.9% accuracy, while GPT‑4 reached about 67.9% accuracy on factual claims. [source](https://originality.ai/blog/what-llm-is-the-most-accurate)
This means that AI will confidently give you answers that are wrong a significant portion of the time.

## 2. Confidence ≠ Accuracy, A Useful Analogy
A good way to understand this is to compare AI behavior to a well‑known politicians:
During the Trump administration, Donald Trump often made statements with very high confidence, even when those statements are shown to be factually incorrect.
Whether you view that as a major problem or a minor one is a matter of opinion.
But the fact remains:
When someone or something, speaks with confidence, it does not guarantee truth.
The same applies to Large Language Models.
They can sound certain, even when they are wrong.
Because of that, you cannot blindly trust everything an LLM says.

## 3. To Check AI’s Work, You Still Need Programming Knowledge
If you rely on AI to generate code, you still need to be able to:

- read it
- understand what it does
- judge whether it’s correct
- spot errors or unsafe logic

Without any programming knowledge, you have no way to verify AI’s output — which can be risky.

## 4. iLogic Makes This Even More Important
For people working with Autodesk Inventor’s iLogic that might be even more important now:

- iLogic rules often involve Inventor-specific logic that AI models don’t fully understand.
- Many iLogic problems require detailed knowledge of your company’s workflow, which AI cannot infer.
- Mistakes in iLogic can lead to broken models, suppressed features, or incorrect automation, problems an untrained user would struggle to detect.

# 5. AI Makes Starting Much Easier
While AI cannot replace the value of understanding, it does make the beginning stages less intimidating.
AI is excellent at:

- giving you a starting point
- explaining confusing topics
- generating examples
- reducing the frustration of the early learning curve
