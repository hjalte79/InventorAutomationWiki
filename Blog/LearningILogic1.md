2. Operators
How to work with data — math operations (+, -, *, /), comparisons (==, >, <), and logic (AND, OR, NOT).

Programs make decisions. if/else statements let your code do different things based on conditions — this is the brain of any program.
4. Loops
Doing something repeatedly without writing it out over and over. The two key types are for loops (repeat a set number of times) and while loops (repeat until a condition changes).

Functions take inputs, do something, and often return an output. This is one of the most powerful concepts in programming.
